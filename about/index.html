About Me

I’m a second-year student interested in software and systems,
learning how things work by building small tools and experiments.

Most of my work so far is focused on low-level programming,
mainly using C, where I explore memory, performance,
and how systems behave under the hood.

At the same time, I’m gradually getting deeper into Python,
using it for scripting, automation, and small practical tools.

I’m also beginning to explore areas around system and information security,
mostly through practice and curiosity rather than formal specialization.

This site is a place where I document what I learn,
experiment with ideas, and share things I build along the way.

Over time, I’d like to contribute to open-source projects
and grow into more complex systems and software.
